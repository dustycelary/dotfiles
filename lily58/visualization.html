What's "timeless" about it

  • Flow Tap (150ms idle window): If you just tapped a letter, the next mod-tap key resolves *instantly* as a tap. Most of your typing never touches the tap-hold timer at all. This is QMK's port of urob's require-prior-idle-ms.
  • Chordal Hold (opposite-hand rule): Same-hand rolls always tap as letters. Mods only activate when the next key is on the opposite hand. Eliminates "as → Cmd+S" and similar errors completely.
  • Long 250ms tapping term + Permissive Hold: Once Flow Tap and Chordal Hold have done their work, the long term means timing rarely decides anything. Cross-hand chords resolve when the second key is pressed-and-released, not when a timer expires.
  • Per-key terms: Pinky mods (A, ;) get 300ms because pinkies are slow. Index mods (F, J) get 220ms because index fingers are fast and precise. The middle ground for ring + middle.

Trade-off vs urob's original

  • urob's 34-key board has no symbol layer — everything is combos. This config keeps the full symbol layer (you have 58 keys, no need to economize) and adds combos *just* for the most-used symbols (parens, braces).
  • The result: faster paren typing without leaving home row, AND a full symbol layer for everything else.
